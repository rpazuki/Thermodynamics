### Carnot — Reflections on the Motive Power of Heat (1824)

*The founding document of thermodynamics. Carnot proves that the maximum efficiency of a heat engine depends only on the temperatures of its reservoirs, not on the working substance — establishing the universality of the Carnot cycle and laying the groundwork for both the First and Second Laws.*

**Source page:** [carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat](wiki/sources/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat.md)

**Chapter summaries:**

| # | Title | Topics |
|---|-------|--------|
| 00 | [Editorial Preface and Biography](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/00-editorial-preface-and-biography.md) | Life of Sadi Carnot, historical context, caloric controversy |
| 01 | [Introduction and the Motive Power Question](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/01-introduction-and-the-motive-power-question.md) | Heat as universal motor, caloric-transport picture, universality question |
| 02 | [The Carnot Cycle and Universality Proof](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/02-the-carnot-cycle-and-universality-proof.md) | Reversible cycle, four-stage description, impossibility argument |
| 03 | [Gas Theory, Specific Heats, and Heat of Expansion](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/03-gas-theory-specific-heats-and-heat-of-expansion.md) | Derivation of universal gas properties, $c_p - c_v$, isothermal heat |
| 04 | [Efficiency, Numerical Estimates, and Practical Engines](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/04-efficiency-numerical-estimates-and-practical-engines.md) | Numerical verification of universality, temperature dependence of efficiency |
| 05 | [Kelvin's Account of Carnot's Theory](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/05-kelvin-account-of-carnots-theory.md) | Carnot coefficient $\mu$, Regnault's data, benchmarking of steam engines |
| 06 | [Appendix A — Unpublished Writings](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/06-appendix-a-unpublished-writings.md) | Carnot's private notebooks, rejection of caloric theory, First Law anticipation |
| 07 | [Appendices B and C — Footnotes and Editorial Notes](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/07-appendices-b-and-c-footnotes-and-editorial-notes.md) | Perpetual motion, adiabatic evidence, Thurston's 1897 update |

**Analysis:** [Logical architecture of the *Réflexions*](wiki/intros/carnot-reflections-motive-power-of-heat/analysis.md) — five-step impossibility proof, caloric as scaffolding, seeds of both laws, legacy through Kelvin and Clausius

### Planck — Treatise on Thermodynamics (1897)

*The definitive phenomenological treatment of classical thermodynamics. Starting from two empirical postulates (impossibility of perpetual motion of the first and second kinds), Planck derives — without any molecular hypothesis — the full architecture of thermodynamics: entropy as a state function, free energy, phase equilibrium, the phase rule, and the thermodynamics of dilute solutions.*

**Source page:** [planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics](wiki/sources/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics.md)

**Chapter summaries:**

| # | Title | Topics |
|---|-------|--------|
| 00 | [Preface and Introduction](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/00-preface-and-introduction.md) | Three historical methods; Planck's phenomenological approach |
| I·1 | [Temperature](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-i-ch-1-temperature.md) | Thermal equilibrium, characteristic equation, perfect and real gases, critical point |
| I·2 | [Molecular Weight](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-i-ch-2-molecular-weight.md) | Avogadro's law, universal gas constant, dissociation |
| I·3 | [Quantity of Heat](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-i-ch-3-quantity-of-heat.md) | Specific heat, calorie, latent heat, refutation of caloric theory |
| II·1 | [First Law — General](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-ii-ch-1-first-law-general.md) | Conservation of energy, Joule's experiments, reversible processes |
| II·2 | [First Law — Homogeneous Systems](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-ii-ch-2-first-law-homogeneous.md) | Internal energy, $c_p - c_v$, adiabatics, Carnot cycle efficiency |
| II·3 | [First Law — Non-homogeneous Systems](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-ii-ch-3-first-law-non-homogeneous.md) | Thermochemistry, heat function, Hess's law, Kirchhoff's equation |
| III·1 | [Second Law — Introduction](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iii-ch-1-second-law-introduction.md) | Irreversibility, direction of change, reversible vs irreversible processes |
| III·2 | [Second Law — Proof](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iii-ch-2-second-law-proof.md) | Entropy theorem from impossibility of perpetual motion of 2nd kind |
| III·3 | [Second Law — Deductions](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iii-ch-3-second-law-deductions.md) | Carnot efficiency, free energy, Gibbs function, direction of processes |
| IV·1 | [Homogeneous Systems](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iv-ch-1-homogeneous-systems.md) | $c_p - c_v$ relation, Joule–Thomson effect, stability conditions |
| IV·2 | [States of Aggregation](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iv-ch-2-states-of-aggregation.md) | Phase equilibrium, Clausius–Clapeyron, Maxwell equal-area rule, triple point |
| IV·3 | [Independent Constituents](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iv-ch-3-independent-constituents.md) | Gibbs phase rule $f = \alpha - \beta + 2$, chemical potentials, general equilibrium |
| IV·4 | [Gaseous Systems](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iv-ch-4-gaseous-system.md) | Entropy of mixing, semipermeable membranes, law of mass action, van't Hoff equation |
| IV·5 | [Dilute Solutions](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/part-iv-ch-5-dilute-solutions.md) | Osmotic pressure, boiling-point elevation, freezing-point depression, van't Hoff law |

**Analysis:** [Conceptual architecture of the *Treatise*](wiki/intros/planck-treatise-on-thermodynamics/analysis.md) — two-postulate structure, three conceptual ladders, entropy as universal currency, gas–solution analogy

### Gibbs — Elementary Principles of Statistical Mechanics (1902)

*The founding text of statistical mechanics. Gibbs derives the laws of thermodynamics from Hamiltonian mechanics via the theory of ensembles — phase space, Liouville's theorem, the canonical distribution, partition function, and the maximum entropy principle — without appeal to molecular models or kinetic theory.*

**Source page:** [gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics](wiki/sources/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics.md)

**Chapter summaries:**

| Ch | Title | Topics |
|----|-------|--------|
| 1 | [General Notions — Conservation of Extension-in-Phase](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-01-general-notions-conservation-extension-in-phase.md) | Phase space, ensembles, Liouville's theorem |
| 2 | [Conservation Applied to Theory of Errors](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-02-conservation-applied-to-theory-of-errors.md) | Gaussian error propagation in phase space |
| 3 | [Conservation Applied to Integration of Equations of Motion](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-03-conservation-applied-to-integration-of-equations-of-motion.md) | Liouville invariant, reduction to quadratures |
| 4 | [The Canonical Distribution](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-04-canonical-distribution.md) | $P \propto e^{-\varepsilon/\Theta}$, modulus as temperature, partition function |
| 5 | [Average Values in the Canonical Ensemble](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-05-average-values-canonical-ensemble.md) | Equipartition $\bar{\varepsilon}_p = \tfrac{1}{2}n\Theta$, Maxwell–Boltzmann distribution |
| 6 | [Extension in Configuration and Velocity](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-06-extension-in-configuration-and-velocity.md) | Coordinate-invariant extensions, factorisation of phase space |
| 7 | [Further Discussion of Averages](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-07-further-discussion-of-averages.md) | Clausius relations, energy fluctuations $\sim 1/n$, large-$n$ limit |
| 8 | [Important Functions of the Energies](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-08-important-functions-of-energies.md) | Phase volume $V(\varepsilon)$, density of states $e^\phi = dV/d\varepsilon$ |
| 9 | [The Function $\phi$ and the Canonical Distribution](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-09-function-phi-and-canonical-distribution.md) | Most probable energy, equivalence of canonical and microcanonical for large $n$ |
| 10 | [Microcanonical Distribution](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-10-microcanonical-distribution.md) | $T \leftrightarrow d\varepsilon/d(\log V)$, $S \leftrightarrow \log V$, Boltzmann formula |
| 11 | [Maximum and Minimum Properties](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-11-maximum-minimum-properties.md) | Canonical distribution minimises average index, maximum entropy principle |
| 12 | [Motion Through Long Periods of Time](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-12-motion-through-long-periods-of-time.md) | Poincaré recurrence, tendency to equilibrium, coarse-grained entropy |
| 13 | [Effect of Various Processes on the Ensemble](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-13-effect-of-various-processes-on-ensemble.md) | Mechanical work vs thermal action, entropy non-decrease, Carnot analogue |
| 14 | [Discussion of Thermodynamic Analogies](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-14-discussion-of-thermodynamic-analogies.md) | Three analogy systems, connection to Planck and Clausius, classical failures |
| 15 | [Systems Composed of Molecules](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/ch-15-systems-composed-of-molecules.md) | Grand ensemble, chemical potentials, Gibbs paradox, entropy of mixing |

**Analysis:** [Four-layer logical structure of the *Elementary Principles*](wiki/intros/gibbs-elementary-principles-statistical-mechanics/analysis.md) — identifications $\Theta \leftrightarrow T$, $-\bar{\eta} \leftrightarrow S$; relation to Planck and Carnot
