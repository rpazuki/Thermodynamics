## Part III — Axiomatic Foundations

### §8 From Carathéodory to Lieb–Yngvason

*The project of placing thermodynamics on a minimal axiomatic basis: Carathéodory's Pfaffian approach (1909), its reception and critique through the twentieth century, and the definitive Lieb–Yngvason programme (1999–2000) that derives entropy purely from the preorder of adiabatic accessibility.*

| Page | Type | Description |
|------|------|-------------|
| [Lieb & Yngvason — A Fresh Look at Entropy (2000)](wiki/sources/lieb-yngvason-fresh-look-entropy.md) | source | Accessible account of the axiomatic programme: entropy as the unique additive order-function on states; adiabatic accessibility preorder; six natural axioms; temperature as a derived concept |
| [Lieb & Yngvason — The Mathematics of the Second Law (GAFA 2000)](wiki/sources/lieb-yngvason-mathematics-second-law.md) | source | Full rigorous treatment: axioms A1–A6, comparison hypothesis derived (not postulated) from thermal contact axioms, explicit entropy construction, positive temperature as a theorem |
| [Pogliani & Berberan-Santos — Carathéodory and Axiomatic Thermodynamics (2000)](wiki/sources/pogliani-caratheodory-axiomatic-thermodynamics.md) | source | Biography of Carathéodory; his two axioms (First Law for adiabatic work; inaccessibility of nearby states); Pfaffian tutorial; reception from Born (1921) through Buchdahl to the Anglo-American school |
| [Redlich — Fundamental Thermodynamics Since Carathéodory (1968)](wiki/sources/redlich-fundamental-thermodynamics-since-caratheodory.md) | source | Critical review: Carathéodory's adiabatic-wall definition of heat; Buchdahl's three-class state partition as improvement; critique of the zeroth law as a rule of order rather than an empirical law |
